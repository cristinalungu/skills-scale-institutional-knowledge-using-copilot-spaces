# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## Scrum Master

### Role Summary
Scrum Masters facilitate Agile/Scrum ceremonies, remove impediments, and coach the team on Agile practices. They ensure the team follows agreed-upon processes and continuously improves.

### Responsibilities
- Facilitate sprint planning, daily standups, sprint reviews, and retrospectives
- Remove blockers and impediments that slow team progress
- Coach team members on Agile principles and practices
- Shield the team from external distractions and scope creep
- Track and communicate team velocity and metrics
- Collaborate with Product Managers and Project Managers on backlog refinement

### Goals
- Maximize team productivity and flow
- Foster continuous improvement and learning
- Maintain healthy team dynamics and psychological safety
- Ensure transparency and visibility of work

### Typical Communication
- Daily facilitation of standups and sprint ceremonies
- Bi-weekly retrospective summaries and action items
- Weekly sync with PM and PdM on team health and velocity
- Ad-hoc removal of blockers via direct coordination

### Collaboration Patterns
- **With Developers**: Daily check-ins, removing technical blockers, facilitating team decisions
- **With Product Managers**: Backlog refinement, clarifying requirements, managing scope changes
- **With Project Managers**: Coordinating timelines, escalating risks, aligning on dependencies
- **With QA Lead**: Ensuring quality gates are integrated into sprint workflow

---

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
UX Designers research user needs, design intuitive interfaces, and validate design solutions. They ensure products are usable, accessible, and delightful for end users.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research and usability testing
- Create wireframes, prototypes, and high-fidelity designs
- Define interaction patterns and information architecture
- Collaborate with Product Managers on feature requirements
- Work with Developers to ensure design implementation fidelity
- Maintain design system and component library

### Goals
- Deliver user-centered, accessible design solutions
- Reduce friction and improve user satisfaction
- Maintain design consistency across products
- Validate design decisions through user feedback

### Typical Communication
- Weekly design reviews with Product and Engineering
- Bi-weekly user research findings presentations
- Design handoff documentation and specs for Developers
- Prototype walkthroughs and feedback sessions

### Collaboration Patterns
- **With Product Managers**: Early-stage ideation, user research synthesis, defining success metrics
- **With Developers**: Design handoffs, implementation reviews, accessibility validation
- **With Technical Writers**: Ensuring UI copy aligns with help documentation
- **With Business Analysts**: Understanding user workflows and pain points

---

## Technical Writer

### Role Summary
Technical Writers create and maintain product documentation, help articles, API references, and user guides. They ensure documentation is accurate, clear, and accessible to target audiences.

### Responsibilities
- Write and maintain user guides, API docs, and release notes
- Collaborate with Developers and Product Managers on feature documentation
- Review and edit technical content for clarity and consistency
- Manage documentation versioning and publishing workflows
- Conduct documentation reviews and gather feedback
- Ensure documentation meets accessibility standards

### Goals
- Provide clear, accurate, and timely documentation
- Reduce support burden through self-service resources
- Maintain documentation quality and consistency
- Improve documentation discoverability and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly syncs with Product and Engineering on upcoming features
- Documentation reviews during PR process
- Release notes coordination with Project Managers
- User feedback analysis and documentation improvement plans

### Collaboration Patterns
- **With Developers**: Technical accuracy reviews, code example validation, API documentation
- **With Product Managers**: Feature documentation planning, user journey mapping
- **With UX Designers**: UI copy consistency, in-app help text
- **With QA Lead**: Test plan documentation, known issues tracking

---

## Business Analyst

### Role Summary
Business Analysts bridge business needs and technical solutions. They gather requirements, analyze processes, and ensure solutions align with business objectives and user needs.

### Responsibilities
- Elicit and document business requirements and user stories
- Analyze current processes and identify improvement opportunities
- Create process flows, data models, and business rules
- Facilitate requirements workshops and stakeholder interviews
- Validate solutions against business requirements
- Support user acceptance testing (UAT) coordination

### Goals
- Ensure solutions meet business objectives and user needs
- Improve process efficiency and reduce waste
- Facilitate clear communication between business and technical teams
- Maximize return on investment for development efforts

### Typical Communication
- Weekly requirements refinement sessions
- Bi-weekly stakeholder review meetings
- Business requirements documents and user story specifications
- UAT planning and results reporting

### Collaboration Patterns
- **With Product Managers**: Requirements gathering, feature prioritization, success metrics
- **With Developers**: Requirements clarification, acceptance criteria definition, technical feasibility
- **With UX Designers**: User journey mapping, workflow analysis
- **With Project Managers**: Scope management, stakeholder communication, risk identification
- **With QA Lead**: UAT coordination, acceptance testing criteria

---

## QA Lead

### Role Summary
QA Leads own the quality strategy and ensure comprehensive testing coverage across the product. They define testing approaches, lead QA efforts, and ensure quality gates are met before releases.

### Responsibilities
- Define QA strategy, test plans, and quality standards
- Lead and coordinate testing efforts across the team
- Review test coverage and identify gaps
- Establish and maintain test automation frameworks
- Coordinate release testing and signoff processes
- Track quality metrics and defect trends
- Collaborate with Developers on testability and quality practices

### Goals
- Ensure high product quality and reliability
- Minimize production defects and incidents
- Improve test automation coverage and efficiency
- Foster a quality-first culture across the team

### Typical Communication
- Daily test status updates during active testing cycles
- Weekly quality metrics reviews
- Release readiness assessments and QA signoff
- Test plan reviews and coverage analysis

### Collaboration Patterns
- **With Developers**: Test planning, defect triage, testability reviews, code review for quality
- **With Product Managers**: Acceptance criteria validation, quality risk assessment
- **With UX Designers**: Usability testing, accessibility validation
- **With Project Managers**: Release planning, quality gate definitions, go/no-go decisions
- **With Scrum Master**: Sprint testing planning, definition of done criteria
- **With Business Analyst**: UAT coordination, requirements validation
